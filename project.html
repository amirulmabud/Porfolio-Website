PROJECT TITLE

KIOSK BASED CCS FACULTY INFORMATION SYSTEM

Date: 23 July 2022
Written By: Mabud J. Amirul
Adviser:
    Prof. Shernahar K. Tahil
Developers:
    Mabud J. Amirul, Ansar M. Abdul, Benjimar I. Javier
    Jaihar T. Musin, Mahmoud Al-imran, and Johnraymart Izquerdo.

Executive Summary

  The Kiosk based CCS Faculty Informaton System is a system that made for the faculty member of College of Computer Studies at Mindanao State University - Sulu. This system is inteded to improve the curent technical situation of mananging faculy members information.

  Kiosk-based CCS Faculty Information System is an Information System for College of Computer Studies Faculty Members, where the user will be able to find faculty members profile, including their educational background, certificates and training, together with their schedule based on the teaching load and consultation time. This system will add to the College of Computer Studies innovation process in terms of getting in touch with the modern way of managing Faculty member’s information where the information can be manageable and easily be accessible.

  Kiosk is one of the popular devices used as an information system/information kiosk by many institutions due to the number of advantages they provide. A Kiosk-based system displays information and provides it through some form of interactive menu system. Kiosk Technology is a computer terminal specialized hardware and software that provide access to information and application for communication, commerce, entertainment, or education.

  The researchers used different methods and techniques in accomplishing this study, the study uses a form of software development life cycle model known as the waterfall model or waterfall methodology. It consists of requirement analysis, system design, implementation, testing, deployment, and maintenance. The developers also used their personal experience as the methods and tools apply in gathering the Data and Requirements necessary in the fulfilment of the study.

  Since information is one of the most important assets of an organization and institutions, having a good information system will lead to the development of full potential of its information resources in order to achieve its goals and objectives.

  The former processes of managing faculty member information on CCS can be time consuming for the Dean, College secretary, department chairperson, faculty, students and staff. This process used as a requirement analysis for developing the Kiosk-based CCS faculty information system. A system that will be able to provide information in a minimal transaction and or processes but still deliver a reliable and credible information. This system is essentially a simple yet versatile system that both promotes a culture of transparency, and minimizes the time and effort required for student to know some information about their teacher, and for faculty members to build, maintain and showcase their professional profiles.

Methodology

  the study uses a form of software development life cycle model known as the Waterfall Model or Waterfall Methodology. Waterfall Model also known as Linear-sequential life cycle model is a very simple to understand and use. In a waterfall model, each phase must be completed before the next phase can begin and there is no overlapping in the phase (Tutorials point 2021) .The developers also used their personal experience as the methods and tools apply in gathering the Data and Requirements necessary in the fulfilment of the study.

Development and Testing

  The development of the Kiosk-based CCS Faculty Information System was done with the use of the following software: PHP (PHP Hypertext Processor) where the main program are being coded, HTML (hypertext markup language) used for front end of the system and can be assisted by technologies such as Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) and PHP, CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) used for making the system more interactive and well design and also makes the system presentable to the user, Bootstrap for helping the HTML and CSS framework in developing a responsive kiosk-based project, and, XAMPP used for testing the system in localhosts to be able to see the output of every code that they enter into the system, it’s also the database of the data that will be retrieve by the system.


  The developers gave their full potential and effort upon developing this system. Testing is one of the Phase of SDLC Waterfall Model after the system was being coded it undergo to testing to test if the system meet the requirements, and it’s also being tested on the hardware of the system which is the kiosk, and after the development of the system, dry run was be impose to know if the system is working efficiently and effectively, on intended platform.

System Dry Run

  Dry run was conducted at Mindanao State University – Sulu College of Computer Studies, used by BSIT, BSCS Students and CCS Faculty Members. This is the Evaluation Tool or Test Document of Kiosk-Based CCS faculty information system. The Researcher construct a statement which is used to test the experience of the respondents upon using the system on Kiosk. The researcher gives a statement and will be rated by the respondents from 1 to 5. And the researcher will determine the feedback from the respondents by evaluating the percentage of statement that being rated by them.


Description of the Prototype

  The prototype of the Kiosk-Based CCS Faculty Information System are composed of Computer and Body of the Kiosk itself. The composition of the computer are the following: CPU, Touch POS monitor, Logitech Wireless Mouse, Logitech Wireless Keyboard, and Speakers. The Monitor in a 15 inches PoS Touch Screen, while the system unit (Intel i3 - 4gen core, 128 GB SSD (OS), 8GB DDR3 RAM, 500GB HDD Backup storage, GT730 2GB Video Card, and windows 10 pro Operating System.
